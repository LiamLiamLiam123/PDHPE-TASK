Youth Health Consultant

School Breakfast Clubs Assessment

Initiative Part 1 Part 2

Chosen Initiative

School Breakfast Clubs A program that gives students food in the morning so they can start the day better.

PART 1: Critiquing the AI

1. Describe the Health Issue

The health issue is students not eating breakfast or eating unhealthy food in the morning.

This matters because if students do not eat breakfast they can feel tired, hungry, and lose focus in class. They may also get grumpy or find it hard to learn. If it happens a lot it can lead to poor health over time.

2. Analyse the AI's Strategies

The AI said to run a Breakfast Club from 7:00 to 7:30 with toast and apples.

In theory this is a good idea because eating before school can help students focus and feel better.

But in real life this would not work well for Year 9 students.

  • Most students will not want to come to school at 7am.
  • Toast and apples is not very exciting.
  • Some students may feel awkward going.
  • Teachers cannot do this every day for long.
  • The room does not feel like a fun place to be.
  • There is nothing fun or social in the plan.

PART 2: The Local Pitch

3. Explain the Relevance to Young People

Many Year 9 students skip breakfast because they are rushed or not hungry in the morning.

At Shellharbour Anglican College this can make students tired and less focused in class. Aswell as continue to keep the students active and full of energy.

This can affect marks, behaviour, and how well students do at school.

4. Discuss your Shellharbour Solution

My idea is a Breakfast Club that is easy to join and not awkward.

It would run a few mornings a week and sometimes at recess so more students can use it.

It would have better food like cereal, yoghurt, fruit, smoothies, and toast with more choices.

Where and when

It would be in the canteen and outside seating areas where students already hang out.

How to get students to come

Make it feel normal and fun. Play music, let friends sit together, and have student leaders help run it. You could also give house points.

Barriers

Some students may think it is only for people who do not have food at home. This can be fixed by telling everyone it is for all students.

Food and money could also be a problem, so the school could ask local shops to help.

Does it help

Yes, because students will have more energy and focus better in class.

It also helps students feel included and more ready for school.
